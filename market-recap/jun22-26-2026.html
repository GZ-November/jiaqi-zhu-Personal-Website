AI De-rating Spreads as Oil Relief Cushions Rotation

Global markets repriced a hawkish Fed and AI leadership break — rotation rather than broad risk-off.

22–26 Jun 2026 Edited by George ZHU Mortise Capital Research

Global markets spent the week repricing a hawkish Fed backdrop and a sharp break in AI leadership. The stress started in U.S. tech: on Tuesday, the Nasdaq fell -2.2%, the S&P 500 lost -1.4%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOXX) dropped -7.9% as investors cut crowded semiconductor exposure.

By Friday, the pressure had spread across the global AI chain: South Korea's KOSPI down -5.8%, Japan's Nikkei off about -4%, and European chip names also lower.

Macro offered little relief. May U.S. headline PCE rose +4.1% YoY and core PCE rose +3.4%, while Q1 GDP was revised up to +2.1% annualized — but consumer spending slowed to just +0.5%.

Rotation, Not Risk-Off

The week still looked more like rotation than a broad risk-off break:

IndexWeekly Change
S&P 500-2.0%
Nasdaq-4.6%
Dow Jones+0.6%
Russell 2000+1.0%

Lower oil prices and easing Treasury yields helped cushion non-tech cyclicals, even as AI-linked equities de-rated globally.

Key Stock: Micron

Micron was the critical name of the week. After falling -13.2% in Tuesday's sell-off, it reported:

  • Fiscal Q3 EPS: $25.11
  • Revenue: $41.46 billion
  • Gross Margin: 84.6%

The earnings confirmed tight AI memory supply — underlying demand for upstream infrastructure remains robust.

Positioning Takeaway

The market's divide is now clearer: upstream memory and components retain pricing power, while consumer hardware and AI applications face higher costs and tougher monetization tests. Positioning should stay selective — profitable AI infrastructure and quality cyclicals over high-multiple AI applications.
